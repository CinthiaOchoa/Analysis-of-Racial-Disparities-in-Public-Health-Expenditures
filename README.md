# Analysis-of-Racial-Disparities-in-Public-Health-Expenditures

## Project Overview

This project investigates whether there is any potential racial bias in the allocation of expenditures for consumers with developmental disabilities. Using data from the **Georgia Department of Behavioral Health and Developmental Disabilities**, the analysis compares spending patterns between **Hispanic** and **White non-Hispanic** consumers across various life stages.

## Key Methodology

  **Data Cleaning:** Processed a dataset of over 600 records using **Python (Pandas, NumPy)**.
  **Outlier Detection:** Applied the **Interquartile Range (IQR)** method to identify and remove invalid entries.
  **Cohort Segmentation:** Categorized consumers into six distinct age groups: 0-5, 6-12, 13-17, 18-21, 22-50, and 51+ years.
  **Statistical Comparison:** Computed average expenditures to identify trends and potential disparities.

## Findings & Visualization

The analysis reveals that expenditure levels are primarily driven by **age-related service needs** rather than ethnic background.


### Observations:

**Age Trend:** Both ethnic groups follow an identical trend where spending increases significantly with age, particularly in the 22-50 and 51+ cohorts.
**Equity:** Average expenditures for Hispanic and White non-Hispanic consumers are nearly the same across most age groups.
**Conclusion:** There is no conclusive evidence of racial discrimination in this dataset. Differences in spending are attributed to the need for more intensive, specialized support and long-term care as consumers age.
